The wheel, an ancient innovation that changed the world. Around 3500 BC, in Mesopotamia, solid wooden disks were used as potter's tools. Spoked wheels emerged around 3000 BC, revolutionizing transportation. They found their way onto chariots and carts, transforming land travel. In 300 BC, water wheels and windmills boosted agriculture. During the Industrial Revolution, wheels powered machines, propelling societies into the modern age. In the 20th century, rubber tires improved transportation, from bicycles to planes. Even in space, wheels played a role as lunar rovers explored the moon during the Apollo missions. Today, wheels are everywhere, enhancing our lives in countless ways. The wheel's legacy is a testament to human ingenuity, connecting our past, present, and future, reminding us that even simple ideas can lead to revolutions.
